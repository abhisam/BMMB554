Course description

This course is designed as a preparation routine for graduate students in Life Sciences. It has several focus areas including evolution of life sciences as well as in-depth overview of sequencing technologies and their applications.

Grading

  • Participation = 50%
  • Final project = 50%

Final project

It will be best if your project is tightly aligned with your actual degree work. Pick a manageable research question that can be answered in a few weeks and does not require extensive data generation. A good alternative is to select a paper on a topic that is interesting for your and try to re-analyze published data in a new light.

DEADLINE: Make a decision by February 1st and discuss it with me by making at appointment.

Topics

We will cover a breadth of topics. Below is the approximate list. Keep in mind that this field is very dynamic. To account for that we may skip or extend some of the subjects.

  1. History: From Genetics to Genomics
  2. Sequencing: From Sanger to Nanopores I
  3. Sequencing: From Sanger to Nanopores II
  4. Algorithms: Alignment Basics
  5. Algorithms: Aligning many sequences quickly
  6. Algorithms: Assembly I
  7. Algorithms: Assembly II
  8. Galaxy: An introduction
  9. Galaxy: Writing your own tools
  10. Managing software with Conda and Bioconda
  11. Jupyter: Scripting responsibly
  12. Re-sequencing I: Introduction and non-diploid case
  13. Re-sequencing II: Diploid genomes
  14. Practicum: Re-sequencing
  15. Transcriptomics I: Refrence-based
  16. Transcriptomics II: Reference-free
  17. Practicum: Transcriptomics
  18. RNA analysis: RiboSeq and ShapeSeq
  19. Practicum: RNA analysis
  20. DNA/Protein interactions I: Approaches
  21. DNA/Protein interactions II: ENCODE Project
  22. Practicum: DNA/Protein interactions
  23. Genome conformation analysis
  24. Practicum: Genome conformation analysis
  25. Metagenomics I: Approaches
  26. Metagenomics II: Community analysis
  27. Practicum: Metagenomics
